Suppose that events A and B each have nonzero probability. Determine whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE.

  • When A and B are independent, it follows that P(A | not B) = P(not B).
  • The addition rule simplifies to P(A and B) = P(A) + P(B) when A and B are mutually exclusive.
  • The joint probability P(A and B) is equal to zero as long as A and B are independent.
  • If A and B are complementary, then P(A) = 1 − P(B).

In a controlled cognitive experiment, participants are asked to press a button as soon as a visual stimulus appears on a screen. Each participant completes many trials. Because reaction time is influenced by many small factors—attention, neural processing speed, fatigue, and momentary distractions—the recorded reaction times vary from trial to trial. Researchers find that reaction times (in milliseconds) for a typical adult under these conditions are approximately normal, with a mean of 250ms and standard deviation of 40ms.

  1. There is roughly a 19% chance that an adult has a reaction time longer than 215ms.
  2. The IQR of reaction times is close to 54ms.
  3. Given that an adult’s reaction time is shorter than 250ms, the probability it is longer than 210ms is approximately 84%
